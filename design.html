Short and long sighted people find it difficult to focus on things they see in various distances

Dyslexic people find it difficult to concentrate on single points of a page

Colour Blind people find it difficult to see the contrast between certain colours

To help this issue larger text on a page will help, it will not make it 100% clear but will be easier for Short and Long Sighted people to read.

White backgrounds with black text are a cause of difficulty when reading for Dyslexic people. To help this using a dark background on white text will make it easier for Dyslexic people to read.

To help this issue high contrast colours will make reading and looking at images easier for Colour Blind people

Visual Impairment Spectrum

There is a spectrum for how visually impaired someone can be, ranging from able to see to blind. Visual Impairment can come from aging, genetics and life circumstances. This tool is important because it allows readability for all users. It lets users modify main body elements on a web page to make it easier for them to read content.

Common Design Practices

Common design practices can make sites unreadable for some users with Visual Impairment. Design Practices that include text formatting such as high tracking or unhyphenated justified text can make reading for dyslexic users very difficult. Another practice is low contrasting elements on a page. This can have an impact on Colour Blind users because they are able to see limited colours and two colours may look the same to them.
